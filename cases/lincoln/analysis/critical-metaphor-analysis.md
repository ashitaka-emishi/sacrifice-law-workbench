# Critical Metaphor Analysis: lincoln

Status: draft, CMT-backed interpretive layer.

## lincoln-01-body-organism

Mapping IDs: lincoln-cmt-001, lincoln-cmt-006

Persuasive function: Makes national survival feel like the preservation of a living body rather than an abstract institutional choice.

Relation to Koenigsbergian analysis: Supports the body-politic corollary more directly than the sacrificial law itself.

Rival readings:

- Conventional body-politic idiom rather than active metaphor; keep conventionality visible in aggregate claims.
- Perish can be a conventional verb for institutional failure; preserve conventionality in claims.

Negative cases:

- The current reviewed sample does not yet show a fully medicalized disease or purification logic.

## lincoln-04-birth-creation

Mapping IDs: lincoln-cmt-005

Persuasive function: Turns war loss into the possibility of renewed political life.

Relation to Koenigsbergian analysis: Complicates preservation by adding rebirth: sacrifice does not merely keep the sacred object alive, it makes renewal imaginable.

Rival readings:

- Republican renewal formula; historical-semantics controls should prevent overstating biological literalness.

Negative cases:

- Current evidence is concentrated in the Gettysburg climax and should not be treated as distributed across the full Lincoln corpus yet.

## lincoln-06-providence-theodicy

Mapping IDs: lincoln-cmt-003, lincoln-cmt-007, lincoln-cmt-008, lincoln-cmt-009

Persuasive function: Places slavery, bloodshed, guilt, and reconciliation under divine judgment rather than partisan triumph.

Relation to Koenigsbergian analysis: Supports historical-sacral framing and guilt distribution, while limiting enemy-destruction claims through reconciliation.

Rival readings:

- Biblical quotation may function as doctrinal citation rather than independently mapped metaphor.
- Dedicated may function as conventional ceremonial diction with limited conceptual force.
- Sunk may be a financial or material idiom without a full metaphorical debt structure.
- Sword remains uncertain: it may be literal weapon, metonym for war, or biblical idiom.

Negative cases:

- Providence language can be doctrinal quotation or rhetorical convention; it should not be used as evidence of private belief without corroboration.

## lincoln-08-sacrificial-death-gift

Mapping IDs: lincoln-cmt-002, lincoln-cmt-004

Persuasive function: Converts soldier death into an offering that obligates the living.

Relation to Koenigsbergian analysis: Directly supports the sacrificial-body dimension, especially in Gettysburg's battlefield setting.

Rival readings:

- Measure may be conventional quantifying language rather than a distinct accounting source domain.
- The sentence may be read as solemn memorial diction rather than a fully active sacrificial mapping.

Negative cases:

- The current evidence sacralizes Union death but does not make killing an end in itself.
