# Critical Metaphor Analysis: lincoln

## lincoln-01-body-organism

Mapping IDs: lincoln-cmt-003, lincoln-cmt-004, lincoln-cmt-005, lincoln-cmt-006, lincoln-cmt-007, lincoln-cmt-008, lincoln-cmt-009, lincoln-cmt-010, lincoln-cmt-011, lincoln-cmt-014, lincoln-cmt-015, lincoln-cmt-017, lincoln-cmt-022, lincoln-cmt-025, lincoln-cmt-029, lincoln-cmt-030, lincoln-cmt-031, lincoln-cmt-032, lincoln-cmt-033, lincoln-cmt-034, lincoln-cmt-035, lincoln-cmt-036, lincoln-cmt-037, lincoln-cmt-038, lincoln-cmt-039, lincoln-cmt-040, lincoln-cmt-041, lincoln-cmt-043, lincoln-cmt-045, lincoln-cmt-046, lincoln-cmt-047, lincoln-cmt-048, lincoln-cmt-049, lincoln-cmt-050, lincoln-cmt-051, lincoln-cmt-052, lincoln-cmt-053, lincoln-cmt-054, lincoln-cmt-056, lincoln-cmt-058, lincoln-cmt-060, lincoln-cmt-062, lincoln-cmt-064

Persuasive function: Makes national survival feel like the preservation of a living body rather than an abstract institutional choice.

Relation to Koenigsbergian analysis: Supports the body-politic corollary more directly than the sacrificial law itself.

Rival readings:

- A nation is not a biological organism, but it is construed as capable of living.
- A polity is not a biological organism, but its political existence is construed as bodily life or death.
- A social phenomenon is treated as a creature with an origin.
- Abstract ambition is repeatedly construed through embodied motion, animal rank, appetite, and fire.
- Abstract civic faculties and memorial expectation are treated through bodily and material processes.
- Abstract danger or political actors are represented as emerging upward like growth.
- Abstract destiny and ambition are represented as attached to an undertaking.
- Abstract freedom is construed as a physical structure with a foundation and biblical fortification imagery.
- Abstract passions and memories are treated as bodies, agents, or lights.
- Ambition and fame are not literal game or crops, but are framed through hunting and agriculture.
- An overseas army is personified as a giant capable of bodily attack.
- Government is abstract-institutional but construed as an entity that can be bane or be annihilated.
- Historical memory is embodied in persons and wounds as if it were a living readable document.
- Human generations and memory are explicitly construed as trees weathered by storms.
- Institutional maintenance is framed through structural support and material decay.
- Passions and judgment are abstract faculties but are treated as agents with temperaments.
- Political destruction is framed through authorship and completion.
- Political institutions are explicitly construed as a built edifice.
- Political liberty and rational support are explicitly construed as architectural materials.
- Political work is construed as construction or demolition of a building.
- Political-legal harm is represented through bodily orientation, falling, and ravaging.
- Public affairs and attention are construed through combat and physical absorption.
- The contextual meaning concerns burial/death rather than ordinary bodily rest after exertion.
- The expression frames exploitation through bodily extraction and biblical labor idiom.
- The nation is not literally born, yet its founding is framed through birth-generation language.
- The nation is treated as an injured body capable of wounds and care.
- The sentence explicitly compares corpses on trees with moss or drapery.
- The world is treated as a remembering person.
- War is not literally a whip, but it is explicitly called a scourge.

Negative cases:

- The current reviewed sample does not yet show a fully medicalized disease or purification logic.

## lincoln-02-covenant-oath

Mapping IDs: lincoln-cmt-020, lincoln-cmt-021, lincoln-cmt-057, lincoln-cmt-059

Persuasive function: Frames the civic relationship as a binding oath, converting political affiliation into solemn obligation.

Relation to Koenigsbergian analysis: Supports the obligatory-frame dimension: citizens are bound by covenant to preserve the sacred object regardless of personal cost.

Rival readings:

- Legal-political reverence is explicitly understood through religious ritual vocabulary.
- The constitutional Union is abstract-political but construed as a separable material entity.

Negative cases:

- Covenant language in Lincoln is often implicit; direct invocations of oath are concentrated in the constitutional oath of office rather than the full corpus.

## lincoln-03-experiment-proposition

Mapping IDs: lincoln-cmt-023, lincoln-cmt-024, lincoln-cmt-028

Persuasive function: Makes the republic's survival feel like an empirical test of democratic self-governance before the world.

Relation to Koenigsbergian analysis: Raises the stakes of sacrifice by making soldier death proof that the democratic proposition is viable rather than merely honorable.

Rival readings:

- A political order is not a laboratory object, but its success is framed as a trial or proof.

Negative cases:

- The experiment frame appears most clearly at Gettysburg; it is less developed in the Second Inaugural, which shifts to providential framing.

## lincoln-05-fathers-inheritance

Mapping IDs: lincoln-cmt-001, lincoln-cmt-002, lincoln-cmt-026, lincoln-cmt-027, lincoln-cmt-042, lincoln-cmt-044

Persuasive function: Constructs political obligation as inherited from founding fathers, making the republic a bequest that the current generation must not squander.

Relation to Koenigsbergian analysis: Supports the sacred-object and obligatory-frame dimensions: the republic is a patrimony whose preservation obligates the heirs.

Rival readings:

- Collective historical existence is explicitly framed as appearing on a stage.
- Events under the sun are not literally a ledger, but the sentence frames history through accounting.
- Memory is not a fortification, and time does not literally fire artillery.
- Reputation and memory are treated through bodily life/death or physical descent.

Negative cases:

- The fathers/inheritance frame in Lincoln typically constructs an all-male genealogy that excludes women and the enslaved from founding agency.

## lincoln-06-providence-theodicy

Mapping IDs: lincoln-cmt-055, lincoln-cmt-061

Persuasive function: Places slavery, bloodshed, guilt, and reconciliation under divine judgment rather than partisan triumph.

Relation to Koenigsbergian analysis: Supports historical-sacral framing and guilt distribution, while limiting enemy-destruction claims through reconciliation.

Rival readings:

- Biblical usage may be conventional theological idiom rather than a separable metaphor.
- The living are not physically set apart like an object or place; they are morally committed to a public task.

Negative cases:

- Providence language can be doctrinal quotation or rhetorical convention; it should not be used as evidence of private belief without corroboration.

## lincoln-07-absence-black-agency

Mapping IDs: lincoln-cmt-063

Persuasive function: Marks a structural gap: the metaphorical systems reviewed raise the question of where Black political and military agency is represented or suppressed.

Relation to Koenigsbergian analysis: Supports the absence-and-agency dimension: the Koenigsbergian framework requires attention to whose agency is suppressed by the dominant metaphor systems.

Rival readings:

- Wealth is not literally submerged; it is imagined as disappearing downward or being consumed.

Negative cases:

- This cluster is currently under-evidenced in the MIPVU review; it marks a review question rather than a confirmed finding.

## lincoln-08-sacrificial-death-gift

Mapping IDs: lincoln-cmt-012, lincoln-cmt-013, lincoln-cmt-016, lincoln-cmt-018, lincoln-cmt-019

Persuasive function: Converts soldier death into an offering that obligates the living.

Relation to Koenigsbergian analysis: Directly supports the sacrificial-body dimension, especially in Gettysburg's battlefield setting.

Rival readings:

- Human death or removal is framed through theatrical stage imagery.
- Revolutionary inheritance and legal liberty are treated as bodily/material things that can be spilled, trampled, or torn.
- The killing is historical violence, not a ritual offering, but is named through sacrificial vocabulary.

Negative cases:

- The current evidence sacralizes Union death but does not make killing an end in itself.
