# Critical Metaphor Analysis: am-rev

Status: draft, CMT-backed interpretive layer.

## am-rev-01-liberty-tyranny

Mapping IDs: amrev-cmt-001, amrev-cmt-005, amrev-cmt-010, amrev-cmt-012, amrev-cmt-017, amrev-cmt-018, amrev-cmt-025, amrev-cmt-026, amrev-cmt-029, amrev-cmt-031, amrev-cmt-043, amrev-cmt-060

Persuasive function: Frames the political choice as an absolute moral binary between freedom and bondage, foreclosing accommodation.

Relation to Koenigsbergian analysis: Sets up the enemy-as-bringer-of-death dimension: the tyrannical enemy threatens the sacred object (liberty) and must be resisted or expelled.

Rival readings:

- Abstract faculties are treated as embodied perceivers or speakers.
- Military and political actions are represented through bodily death-work and national embodiment.
- Paine applies coercive bodily restraint vocabulary to constitutional and imperial dependence.
- Paine uses physical mortality to explain the social necessity of cooperation.
- The war is literal, but the phrase condenses political destruction into conventional elemental and bodily imagery.

Negative cases:

- The liberty/tyranny binary suppresses moderate constitutional arguments; not all colonial writers used this frame with equal intensity.

## am-rev-02-people-republic

Mapping IDs: amrev-cmt-002, amrev-cmt-003, amrev-cmt-004, amrev-cmt-006, amrev-cmt-011, amrev-cmt-014, amrev-cmt-015, amrev-cmt-019, amrev-cmt-020, amrev-cmt-022, amrev-cmt-023, amrev-cmt-040, amrev-cmt-041, amrev-cmt-042, amrev-cmt-045, amrev-cmt-046, amrev-cmt-047, amrev-cmt-048, amrev-cmt-049, amrev-cmt-050, amrev-cmt-051, amrev-cmt-052, amrev-cmt-053, amrev-cmt-056, amrev-cmt-057, amrev-cmt-058

Persuasive function: Constitutes a sovereign people and a republic as natural political entities that precede and authorize the revolutionary act.

Relation to Koenigsbergian analysis: The people as sacred political object is the object that sacrifice preserves; it requires the enemy-as-threat framing to motivate action.

Rival readings:

- Legal-political entities do not literally suffer bodily injury or exposure.
- Political colonies, pamphlets, and founding moments are not literal parents, infants, worlds, or births.
- Some war deaths are literal, but Paine's rhetoric gives them sacrificial political meaning.

Negative cases:

- 'The people' in this corpus is structurally exclusive; the claim to universal sovereignty coexists with enslaved, Indigenous, and propertyless exclusion.

## am-rev-03-founding-sacrifice

Mapping IDs: amrev-cmt-059, amrev-cmt-061

Persuasive function: Transforms the costs of revolution into sacred offerings that obligate posterity and legitimate the founding.

Relation to Koenigsbergian analysis: Directly activates the sacrificial-body dimension: the revolutionary dead create the sacred debt that sustains the republic.

Rival readings:

- The pledge is a real political act, but the closing formula gives it sacred and sacrificial force.

Negative cases:

- Founding sacrifice language is concentrated in Washington's valedictory orders; the Declaration and Common Sense use it less directly.

## am-rev-04-providence-covenant

Mapping IDs: amrev-cmt-035, amrev-cmt-036, amrev-cmt-037

Persuasive function: Sacralizes the revolutionary cause by placing it under divine sanction and framing it as a covenant with providential history.

Relation to Koenigsbergian analysis: Supports the historical-sacral framing; the cause is not merely political but divinely backed, heightening the stakes of sacrifice.

Rival readings:

- Royal authority is political, but Paine frames it through religious transgression and bodily mortality.

Negative cases:

- Providence language in Washington's orders is formulaic and military; it should not be treated as evidence of deep theological conviction without further corroboration.

## am-rev-05-virtue-corruption

Mapping IDs: amrev-cmt-027

Persuasive function: Frames the revolution as a moral regeneration against the corruption that monarchy and luxury produce.

Relation to Koenigsbergian analysis: Supports the enemy-as-contamination framing; corruption is a moral and civic disease spread by the monarchical enemy.

Rival readings:

- Social formation and moral purity are abstract but are explained through physical force and fortification.

Negative cases:

- Virtue/corruption discourse is most prominent in Common Sense and less developed in the military orders or Declaration.

## am-rev-06-union-founding-memory

Mapping IDs: amrev-cmt-007, amrev-cmt-008, amrev-cmt-009, amrev-cmt-013, amrev-cmt-016, amrev-cmt-021, amrev-cmt-024, amrev-cmt-028, amrev-cmt-030, amrev-cmt-032, amrev-cmt-033, amrev-cmt-034, amrev-cmt-038, amrev-cmt-039, amrev-cmt-044, amrev-cmt-054, amrev-cmt-055

Persuasive function: Creates an obligation to preserve the founding order by framing it as a sacred legacy held in trust for posterity.

Relation to Koenigsbergian analysis: Sustains the sacred-object dimension over time; the union becomes the object that must be preserved across generations.

Rival readings:

- Political collectives and constitutions are abstract institutions, not literal bodies or machines.
- Political development is abstract but is construed as organic growth.
- Political oppression and institutional remedies are abstract but are framed through physical subjection and protection.
- The relation among peoples and states is political, not a literal band or substance.

Negative cases:

- Union/founding-memory framing is concentrated in Washington's 1783 orders; it is prospective and valedictory, not a description of the war's motivations.
