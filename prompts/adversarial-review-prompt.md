# Adversarial Review Prompt

## Purpose

Stress-test a methodology section, annotation set, analysis artifact, or draft
claim by looking for unsupported inference, rival explanations, and failure
modes.

## Inputs

- Artifact under review.
- Relevant methodology section.
- Source, annotation, concordance, analysis, or validation artifacts.
- Corpus scope and intended readiness status.

## Review Stance

Act as a skeptical but fair scholarly reviewer. Look for the strongest version
of the project, but do not protect weak claims. Prefer precise qualifications
over blanket rejection when the underlying evidence is useful.

## Workflow

1. Summarize what the artifact is claiming.
2. Identify the evidence actually supplied.
3. Separate evidence, interpretation, inference, speculation, and open
   questions.
4. Test rival explanations: register, genre, legal formula, religious
   convention, translation artifact, source selection, chronology, and prompt
   bias.
5. Check whether absence claims state search scope and presence criteria.
6. Check whether psychological interpretation avoids diagnosis and private
   mind-reading.
7. Recommend concrete revisions.

## Output

Return:

- strongest supported version of the claim;
- unsupported or overextended claims;
- rival explanations that must be preserved;
- evidence or validation still needed;
- recommended readiness status;
- revision checklist.

## Quality Criteria

The review should help the project become more defensible. Do not merely say a
claim is speculative; identify what evidence would make it stronger or what
qualification would make it honest.
